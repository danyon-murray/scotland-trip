Park & Ride / bus and trains for Edinburgh 
🅿️ Edinburgh Park & Ride Options

These are convenient parking spots just off main roads into Edinburgh, with free or low-cost parking and reliable bus/tram connections into the city centre.

⸻

1️⃣ Ferrytoll Park & Ride (Fife side)
	•	Location: Close to Charlestown / M90
	•	Parking: Free (including overnight)
	•	Bus into city centre: Stagecoach routes X54 / X55 / X58 / X59 / X60
	•	Fares: ~£7–£10 return per adult
	•	Travel time: ~45–50 min
	•	Notes:
	•	Ideal for fast, direct bus access from Fife side
	•	Express buses run every ~20 min Mon–Sat; ~30 min on Sundays
	•	Peak times (rush hours) buses may run every 5–15 min
	•	Typical Service Hours: 06:00–22:00 (varies slightly on Sundays)

⸻

2️⃣ Ingliston Park & Ride (West Edinburgh)
	•	Location: Near A90/A8 approach from Queensferry Crossing
	•	Parking: Free (~1,000+ spaces)
	•	Tram into city centre:
	•	Day ticket: ~£4.50 per adult
	•	Journey: ~30–35 min to Princes Street or St Andrew Square
	•	Frequency: Every 7–10 min during peak times
	•	Notes:
	•	Tram stop is directly at the car park entrance
	•	Stops at each tram station along the way (not express)

⸻

Both are convenient; Ferrytoll is slightly faster if coming from Charlestown, Ingliston is ideal if approaching via Queensferry Crossing.

Other Park & Ride Options (Free / Low-Cost)
	•	Straiton, Hermiston, Sheriffhall, Wallyford – Free parking, bus connections (~£5 day ticket)
	•	Newcraighall – Cheap £0.50 parking, rail/bus into centre

Tips
	•	Ferrytoll buses are express services, but route numbers may vary (X55, X58, X59, X60, etc.)
	•	Ingliston tram is frequent and reliable; perfect for avoiding city centre parking hassle
	•	Bring change or card for bus/tram tickets
	•	Check peak times for faster frequency if traveling during rush hours
	•	Park & Ride lots open 24 hours, but staff mostly present early morning to late evening
